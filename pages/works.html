  2. Applications of forcing: Suslin's problem, written for the Part III Essay course as part of my MASt Mathematics degree at the University of Cambridge.
    Supervised by Benedikt Löwe.
    (May 2025)
    Does there exist a non-empty complete dense unbounded linear order in which every collection of pairwise disjoint open intervals is at most countable? Certainly. The real line is one such linear order. Are there any others? This was a question which was asked by Mikhail Suslin in 1919. It took till 1971 to show that ZFC can neither prove nor disprove the existence of any such linear orders other than the real line, unless ZFC is inconsistent. We build up to this independence result by Robert Solovay and Stanley Tennenbaum, using the technique of forcing.

  7. A suite on the Wasserstein metric.
    Supervised by Josephine Evans.
    (September 2024)
    This is an exploration of several properties of the Wasserstein 1-distance, which is a way of measuring distances between probability measures. We limit our attention to probability measures on the real line to simplify notations as well as obtaining further properties, though a lot of the results can be generalised.

  8. Cichoń's diagram and the rearrangement number, written for the MA395 Essay module as part of my BSc Mathematics degree at the University of Warwick.
    Supervised by Adam Epstein and András Máthé.
    (April 2024)
    This is an introduction to several cardinal characteristics of the continuum. In particular, we discuss the cardinals which appear in Cichoń's diagram and the rearrangement number, which stemmed from a MathOverflow post. This essay is mainly about proving several inequalities between these cardinals in ZFC.
